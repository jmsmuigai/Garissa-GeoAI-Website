A Promise in the Floodwaters: How a Cholera Outbreak Forged My Vision for a GeoAI-Powered Garissa

By James Mukoma, Director of ICT, Garissa County & GeoAI Consultant

I remember the feeling vividly: immense, paralyzing frustration. It was a frustration sharpened by the knowledge that we could do better. A flash flood in a remote sub-county had triggered a cholera outbreak, and as the Director of ICT for Garissa County, I was at the heart of the response. The first reports trickled in from a Community Health Worker via a standard phone call, days after the initial cases.

The reality of our response was a painful lesson in the cost of outdated systems.

We were fighting a 21st-century health crisis with 20th-century tools. My team and I manually plotted case locations on a static, out-of-date map. We dispatched a physical team, their journey agonizingly slow over impassable roads. There was no centralized digital system to track cases in real-time, no way to monitor the spread, no method to instantly coordinate between the health, water, and disaster management departments. We were reacting to old information, and every delay had a human cost.

For the citizens, the situation bred fear and desperation. For the frontline health workers, it was a sense of being overwhelmed and unsupported. For my team, it was the anguish of knowing we had the GIS skills and technical expertise to build a better response, but the tools and systems weren't in place. We were fighting with one hand tied behind our backs. That experience didn’t just challenge me; it fundamentally changed me. It solidified my mission.

From Reaction to Prediction: The GeoAI Imperative

In the days that followed, a single question burned in my mind: What if we could have seen it coming?

That moment starkly illustrated that without reliable, real-time data and connected systems, public administration, no matter how dedicated its people, will always be a step behind a crisis. This is where my work in GeoAI—the fusion of Geospatial science and Artificial Intelligence—transforms from a technical specialty into a moral imperative.

Imagine a different scenario, powered by an integrated system:

An initial health alert from a CHW’s simple smartphone app is instantly plotted on a dynamic, shared map. An AI model, fed with real-time rainfall data, historical outbreak patterns, and population density, immediately flags the area as a high-risk hotspot for cholera spread. Automated alerts are simultaneously pushed to the Water department to initiate water source testing and to the Logistics team to pre-position essential supplies like chlorine tablets and rehydration salts.

This isn’t science fiction. This is the power of a connected "system of systems" that turns raw data into life-saving decisions. This is the vision for the ASAL Integrated Resource Management (IRM) Platform I am building.

Automating Hope: AI Agents for the Frontline

But we must go further. It’s not just about building better dashboards for managers; it’s about empowering every single person in the response chain. This is where my focus on Applied AI and custom AI agents comes in.

Think about the workflow of a public health officer on the ground. How can AI serve them directly?

"Good morning, Aisha. Based on yesterday's rainfall and new case reports, the AI predicts a 75% probability of cluster spread in the Iftin ward. I have highlighted the three most vulnerable households on your map and drafted a supply request for your approval. Would you like me to dispatch it?"

This is how we transform personal workflows. We use AI not to replace human expertise, but to augment it, freeing up our frontline heroes to do what they do best: care for our communities. By building these secure, custom AI agents within our own Google Cloud environment, we ensure the highest levels of data privacy while delivering unprecedented efficiency.

A Call to Co-Create Our Future

The lessons from that flood were written in the frustration and resilience of our community. They taught me that our greatest challenge in the ASAL regions is not a lack of data, but a failure to connect it to timely action.

My work is dedicated to building those connections. As a GeoAI specialist and as a public servant, my mission is to build the intelligent, automated systems that will ensure we are never again caught so unprepared. The ASAL IRM Platform is more than a project; it is a promise to our people that we will harness the best of technology to build a safer, more resilient, and sustainable future for Garissa and beyond.

I am seeking development partners, NGOs, and innovators who share this vision. Let's work together to turn the lessons of the past into the solutions of tomorrow.
